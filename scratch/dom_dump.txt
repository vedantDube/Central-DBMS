</span><h5 class="sc-fzokOt kxGmNr"><span>1. Do I need both </span><code><span>x-api-key</span></code><span> and </span><code><span>Authorization: Bearer</span></code><span>?</span></h5><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: Yes. Both headers are </span><strong class="sc-fzoXzr bUnOKm"><span>mandatory</span></strong><span>.</span><br><code><span>x-api-key</span></code><span> identifies the client.</span><br><code><span>Authorization</span></code><span> validates the session/JWT.</span><br><span>Missing either header = </span><strong class="sc-fzoXzr bUnOKm"><span>401 Unauthorized</span></strong><span>.</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>2. Can I generate an X-API-Key from a sub-user account?</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: No. The X-API-Key </span><strong class="sc-fzoXzr bUnOKm"><span>can only be generated from the Primary Seller Account</span></strong><span>. Sub-users have no permission to create or rotate keys.</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>3. Can I regenerate the key if it’s compromised?</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: Yes. Click </span><strong class="sc-fzoXzr bUnOKm"><span>Generate X-API-KEY</span></strong><span> again, and the old key becomes invalid instantly. Make sure the new key is updated in all your systems.</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>4. What is the default rate-limit tier?</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: Every new X-API-Key starts in the </span><strong class="sc-fzoXzr bUnOKm"><span>Default tier</span></strong><span>, which is intentionally low so you can test safely without impacting production.</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>5. Is the rate limit applied per key or per user?</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: Rate limiting is applied per X-API-Key, not per user or per token.</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>6. Does the X-API-Key expire?</span></p><span>
</span><p><span>Answer: No. It doesn’t auto-expire. But if regenerated manually, the previous one becomes invalid.</span></p><span>
